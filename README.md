# What is it??

Behold, through a socket server running in Python, here's a simple chat that anyone can access easily on their network!

# Try it out!

Upon running it, you'll be met with the homescreen:
<img width="804" height="427" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 5 18 15 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/21e88bad-730c-4efb-9f05-081c43440e14" />

There are two computers interacting locally on a network through each chat: the server, and the client.

The server hosts the chat, by clicking "Host a Local Chat" in the menu.

Once they have clicked it, they will be met with this screen.
<img width="804" height="427" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 5 20 32 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ca536b6e-23dc-4e8d-9ddd-09062689fa6f" />

On the client side, click "Join a Local Chat".

Once they have clicked it, they will be met with this screen:
<img width="804" height="427" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 5 21 48 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/40adfd9a-abc0-40d7-99e7-5ad164077514" />

Enter the IP from the host side:
<img width="804" height="427" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 5 22 35 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/98b7e3ac-f99b-4d34-86b6-7fc996052049" />

Then, enter the port:


<img width="804" height="427" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 5 23 22 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c9e30772-933c-4516-8a8e-f898ee69dc1e" />
